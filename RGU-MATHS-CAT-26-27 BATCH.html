Semester I Paper Structure

The 2026–29 B.Sc. Mathematics first semester includes three MAT core papers and two CAT-oriented papers. The portal focuses on the two CAT papers while positioning them as skill bridges for the core mathematics papers.

Course TypePaperRole in SemesterCAT Skill Link
MATClassical AlgebraCore conceptual mathematicsSupported by algebra visualization and function behaviour.
MATTrigonometry, Vector Calculus & Fourier SeriesCore mathematical analysisSupported by trigonometric visualization and wave modelling.
MATCalculusCore differential and integral thinkingSupported by derivative, tangent, area and optimization applets.
CATStatistical Foundation of Data Analytics – IPractical analytics foundationBuilds data collection, visualization, descriptive statistics, correlation and regression.
CATGeoGebra for Algebra, Trigonometry, CalculusPractical visualization foundationBuilds dynamic mathematical visualization and model-based understanding.

Assessment Model

Each assessment is a 90-minute individual GeoGebra practical with 5 problems from easy, medium and hard levels.

Technical / Mathematical Accuracy30Correct computation, graph, interpretation or model.
Tool Usage20Effective use of prescribed software/lab tool.
Visualization / Analysis20Clear chart, model, table or interpretation.
Process Documentation15Step-by-step worksheet, file, screenshot or report.
Viva / Reflection15Student explains what was done, why it works and where it is useful.
